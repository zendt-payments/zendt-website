(06) · FAQ Common questions answered

Everything you need to know.

Clear answers about fees, currencies, settlement, and how Zendt works for Indian freelancers receiving from global clients.

Zendt Payments is international payments infrastructure built for Indian freelancers and independent earners. It lets you share a simple payment link with any client worldwide, receive in their local currency, and have the funds settled into your Indian bank account in INR — at a flat 4% platform fee with no hidden charges or FX markups.

Zendt charges a flat 4% platform fee on every international receipt, plus 18% GST on that fee. That is it. No SWIFT correspondent bank fee, no FX markup on the live mid-market rate, and no monthly subscription. On ₹1,00,000 received: you pay ₹4,000 fee + ₹720 GST = ₹4,720 total. You keep ₹95,280.

International payments settle to your Indian bank account in T+3 business days from the time your client pays. Domestic UPI payments settle in T+1 (next business day). Traditional bank wires typically take 5–7 days and cost significantly more.

The GCC corridor (UAE, Saudi Arabia, Qatar, Kuwait, Bahrain, Oman) is one of the most significant sources of income for Indian freelancers, yet most competing platforms do not support it. Zendt fully supports AED, SAR, QAR and all GCC currencies, alongside USD, GBP, EUR, AUD, CAD, SGD, and 140+ more.

Traditional bank wires charge SWIFT fees (₹500–1,500), correspondent bank fees (₹1,000–3,000), and a 2–4% hidden FX markup on top of the declared exchange rate. Combined, you lose 6–9% or more. Zendt is a flat 4% with no FX markup, no paperwork, and no bank visits — just a link your client clicks.
